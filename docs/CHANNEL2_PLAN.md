## 1. Executive verdict

**Launch an English-language, serialized cinematic nature-documentary
channel set on ONE persistent, original alien planet.** Attenborough-grammar
episodes ("Planet Earth" for a world that doesn't exist), every creature
obeying real evolutionary biology and physics, openly labeled speculative
fiction. Working concept name: **the living-atlas format** — the channel IS
the planet; each episode documents one biome, species, or planetary event,
and the world's map, timeline and food web grow with the library.

Why this wins: it sits at the exact intersection of (a) violently proven
demand, (b) production bottlenecks that killed every incumbent, which our
pipeline removes, and (c) original IP that compounds — the moat is the world
itself, not the tooling.

## 2. Evidence (verified vs judgment)

**Verified:**
- Alt Shift X's *All Tomorrows* retelling: **3.8M views in one month, 4.8M+**
  — a 40-minute speculative-evolution documentary went mainstream and
  spawned a lasting meme subculture. Demand for this material is not niche.
- Curious Archive built a fast-growing channel doing nature-doc breakdowns
  of alien planets and alternate Earths — but almost entirely covering
  OTHER people's worlds (games, books, films). Growth proves the format;
  the dependency proves the gap (original-world position unoccupied at
  cinematic quality).
- Biblaridion's *Alien Biospheres*: an episodic ORIGINAL alien planet with
  fierce returning-viewer behavior (fan theories, TV Tropes pages, forum
  threads) — but months between episodes because manual 3D is the
  bottleneck. Serialized worldbuilding retains; production speed fails.
- Platform context 2026: YouTube's "inauthentic content" policy punishes
  low-variation AI spam, while directed, researched, cinematic AI formats
  are the current growth wave (e.g. the AI time-traveller trend covered by
  The Guardian, May 2026). Quality-directed AI is an advantage; lazy AI is
  a policy risk.

**Judgment (flagged):** the speculative-evolution audience skews global,
young, high-retention, and is starved between incumbent uploads; a weekly
cadence at cinematic quality would be structurally impossible for manual
creators but routine for our pipeline. No incumbent owns "one persistent
world, weekly, photoreal nature-doc grammar."

## 4. Five finalists — stress test

**#1 Alien planet atlas** — 100+ episodes trivially (biomes × species ×
events × deep-time epochs); 5-second comprehension ("nature docs from
another world"); every episode = one creature hero shot thumbnail; Shorts =
60s field-guide entries; grows STRONGER with library (map/timeline
cross-references drive binge); copyable mechanics but not the accumulated
world — the IP defends itself. PASS, best-in-class on every test.
**#2 Recovered expeditions** — strong but horror-adjacent tone limits
advertiser pool; credibility devices risk "is this real?" moderation issues
without careful labeling. PASS with caveats → make it Series 2 inside #1's
universe (expedition framing) instead of its own channel.
**#3 Impossible engineering** — Hoog/megaprojects competition rising;
per-episode research heavier; weaker returning-viewer glue (no persistent
world). CONDITIONAL PASS.
**#4 Post-human Earth** — one long timeline risks exhausting; heavy overlap
with existing All Tomorrows meme space (derivative perception). CONDITIONAL.
**#5 Alternate-history newsreels** — demand proven (AlternateHistoryHub) but
saturated commentary space; real-figure/real-event sensitivities. MARGINAL.

## 5. Comparison with Terra Incognita (channel 1)

Different language (English vs Hindi), different promise (fictional world vs
real science), different grammar (serene Attenborough awe vs urgent
simulation countdown) → near-zero brand confusion, near-zero audience
cannibalization. **Same pipeline runs both**: script_gen prompt pack, TTS
engine (new ElevenLabs/other English voice), Remotion packs (new style),
assets (AI-first instead of stock-first — the inversion is the main change),
QC/factcheck (repurposed as canon-consistency checking), release flow —
all reused. Shorts stay strictly on their own channel.

## 6. Final recommendation — the package

1. **Positioning:** cinematic nature documentaries from one persistent,
   scientifically rigorous fictional planet; openly speculative fiction.
2. **Promise:** "Every week, a real documentary from a world that doesn't
   exist."
3. **Signature visual identity:** photoreal AI stills + parallax + selective
   Wan/fal motion for hero behavior shots; one planet-wide art bible (two
   suns? one moon? fixed palette per biome); recurring cartographic
   interludes (the growing atlas); field-log typography; NO glass-HUD
   (that's channel 1's language).
4. **Narrator:** measured, warm, wonder-first English voice (ElevenLabs or
   similar; pick once, never change) — David-Attenborough grammar, zero
   hype-speak.
5. **Episode structure (7–9 min):** cold open on one creature in trouble
   (0:00–0:25) → biome establishment + atlas locator (0:25–1:10) → three
   escalating survival mechanisms, each a mini-payoff (1:10–5:30, re-hooks
   at each) → ecosystem twist: how the mechanisms interlock (5:30–7:00) →
   canon-stinger: one unexplained detail teasing next episode (7:00–7:40).
6. **Shorts (30–40s):** single field-guide entry — one creature, one
   impossible-but-plausible adaptation, loop-friendly; related-video link
   to the parent episode.
7. **Cadence:** 1 long-form + 3 Shorts weekly. Never faster until 10
   episodes hold retention.
8. **Ten name candidates:** Ecos of Elsewhere · The Verdant Archive ·
   Atlas Unknown · Worldfall · The Faunary · Otherworld Field Guide ·
   Planet Never · Chlorophyll & Iron · The Speculative Atlas · Last
   Landfall. (Check handle/trademark availability before choosing.)
9. **Tagline:** "Field documentaries from a world that never was."
10. **Viewer participation:** community-post polls vote on which unexplained
    canon-stinger gets investigated next; commenters who propose adaptations
    that survive the science check get species named after them — cheap,
    powerful returning-viewer glue.
11. **Required pipeline changes:** English voice adapter; AI-first asset
    priority (stock demoted to reference only); a `canon.json` world bible
    (species, biomes, physics constants, naming rules) that script prompts
    and the factcheck stage validate against (factcheck becomes
    canon-consistency + real-science plausibility check); one new Remotion
    style pack + atlas/map component; species-consistency via
    seed+reference-image reuse in fal. Est. 2–3 sessions of work.
12. **Cost/time per episode:** ~15–25 AI stills ($0.75–1.50 FLUX) + 2–4 Wan
    motion shots ($0.50–1.50) + voice (~$1–3 ElevenLabs, or less on
    alternatives) ≈ **$3–6 per long-form**, ~$0.50/Short; 60–120 min
    unattended + your ~15-min review. Meaningfully pricier than channel 1
    (₹20–90) but still trivial vs any human alternative.
13. **Human review:** same gates as channel 1 (watch before publish; canon
    vetoes; thumbnail pick). Plus: you own the world bible — taste decisions
    about what exists on the planet are permanently yours.
14. **Monetization path:** AdSense (English CPM 3–8× Hindi) → memberships
    (atlas wallpapers, canon deep-dives) → the real prize: the world as IP
    (book/print field guide à la All Tomorrows, game/licensing upside).
15. **Main risks:** AI-slop perception (mitigate: art bible, motion
    restraint, research-real-biology depth, visible sourcing of the science);
    species visual consistency across episodes (hardest technical problem —
    mitigate with reference-image conditioning + limit recurring species);
    format fatigue (mitigate with series rotation: biomes / expeditions /
    planetary events); YouTube inauthentic-content policy (mitigate: heavy
    human direction, unique per-episode research, open fiction labeling in
    every description).

## 8. Pilot blueprint — "The Tide That Thinks"

Titles: (a) This Ocean Is One Animal (b) The Tide That Thinks (c) A Sea
That Hunts Together. Thumbnail: bioluminescent swarm forming a single
colossal silhouette beneath a small drifting creature; text "IT'S ONE
ANIMAL". Cold open (0:00–0:25): a grazer feeding at dusk; the water behind
it starts to organize. Structure: swarm intro → how a distributed mind
evolves (real: slime molds, siphonophores cited) → hunting sequence (Wan
motion shot #1) → the twist: the swarm farms its prey rather than
exterminating it → atlas zoom-out + stinger ("something inland has learned
to poison the tide"→ ep 5 hook). Assets: ~20 stills (5 biome, 8 creature,
4 swarm-scale, 3 atlas), 3 Wan clips (hunt, swarm-shape, dusk establishing),
2 diagram interludes (distributed-cognition explainer, food-web card).
Sound: slow synth-orchestral bed, hydrophone ambience, swarm = granular
shimmer motif (this becomes the planet's recurring sound). Cost ≈ $4–5.
Checkpoints: canon.json review → script/science review → thumbnail pick →
final watch.

## 9. Validation before committing to 30 episodes

Produce **3 pilots only** (ep 1, 2, 3). Publish weekly with full Test &
Compare and 3 Shorts each. Targets after 3 weeks: browse CTR ≥ 4%; 30s
retention ≥ 65%; average % viewed ≥ 50% (7-min runtime); subs per 1,000
views ≥ 4; ≥ 10 comments/video engaging with the WORLD (asking canon
questions = the killer signal). **Continue** if ≥3 targets hit or clearly
trending up. **Pivot format** (shorter episodes / expedition framing /
different biome emphasis) if retention passes but CTR fails or vice versa.
**Abandon** if after 6 videos nothing beats channel-1 baseline and comments
show zero world-curiosity. Honest odds (judgment): ~30–35% strong traction
by day 90, ~40% slow-build worth continuing, ~25–30% kill — the upside
(compounding IP, English CPM, no incumbent) justifies the 3-pilot bet at
<$20 total risk.

## 10. Roadmaps

**30 days:** pipeline changes (§6.11) → canon.json v1 (one ocean biome +
6 species, that's all) → 3 pilots produced & published weekly → validation
readout. **90 days:** if green — episodes 4–13, atlas page in channel

named after a commenter, evaluate Wan budget increase for 1 extra motion
shot/episode; decision point on print field-guide teaser as community

## 11. Go/no-go

**GO — via the 3-pilot validation gate, not a 30-video commitment.** The
demand evidence is real, the gap is real, the pipeline advantage is real,
and the whole bet costs less than $20 and three weeks to falsify.
